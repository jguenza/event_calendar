Seven-Dish Christmas Dinner

Date 24 December 2025 Format Seven courses Mode Read-only (GitHub Pages)

A Belgian and a Northeastern Brazilian walk into a seven-course dinner with two Dutch sisters—what could possibly go wrong?
This is exactly how international diplomacy works—one plate at a time.

Dish 1
Welcome

Panettone met Koffie & Thee alla Gezelligheid

Pistachio panettone from Sicily meets Christmas bread from the Roman bakery in Elst—served with coffee, tea, and zero respect for borders.

Dish 2
After-walk

After-Walk Fraises au Chocolat de Belgique

Belgian strawberries in Dutch chocolate, paired with demi-sec Champagne de Castellane—because the walk was long, and discipline has limits.

Dish 3
Game break

Pequeño Recuerdo de España

During this course, we play Take 5—the ladies are the favorites, and the gentlemen are playing for dignity alone. A small salute to Spain—olives filled with anchovies and crisp chips, simple, salty, and made for sharing. Served with a selection of non-alcoholic spritz and non-alcoholic cava—Spanish spirit, clear heads, and zero excuses.

Dish 4
Sea + elegance

Décadence Nordestine à la Thermidor

Lobster in classic Thermidor sauce, green asparagus wrapped with king crab tapenade—Northeastern abundance dressed in French elegance. Paired with Anjou Blanc La Grande Croix—a white that bridges sea and land, gently preparing the palate for what follows next.

Dish 5
Passage

Passage Terre–Mer, Brie à la Truffe

Truffled soft brie with bread, still paired with Anjou Blanc La Grande Croix—the official crossing where seafood shakes hands with the land and nobody notices the border.

Dish 6
Wild Tuscany

Wild Stufato alla Toscana

Wild pasta served with slowly cooked wild boar and deer—hunted by tradition, tamed by patience, and finished without mercy. Served with Syrah À Deux Pas (2018), grown just two steps away from France’s most expensive Syrah vineyards—proof that proximity can be destiny.

Dish 7
Finale

Tiramisù Classico

The proper Italian ending—classic tiramisù served with English tea, because even traditions enjoy a polite conversation.
